Tips & Tricks for a Productive Allotment

These tips draw on common gardening wisdom and research‑based advice to help you get the most from your plot.

  • Rotate your crops: Grow vegetables from different plant families in different beds each year to minimise pest and disease build‑up and maintain soil fertility.
  • Feed the soil: Add plenty of compost or well‑rotted manure to your beds each winter to improve structure and fertility. Healthy soil leads to healthy plants.
  • Sow little and often: For crops like lettuce, radishes and carrots, sow small batches every few weeks so you have a continuous supply rather than a glut all at once.
  • Use companion planting: Plant flowers such as marigolds and nasturtiums to attract pollinators and beneficial insects and to deter pests like aphids and whitefly.
  • Harvest regularly: Picking fruits and vegetables when they’re ready encourages plants to produce more. Beans, courgettes and cucumbers, for example, will stop cropping if left to mature fully on the plant.
  • Protect against extreme weather: Use cloches, fleece or polytunnels to protect seedlings from late frosts and to extend the season for tender crops. Provide shade and mulch in hot weather to conserve moisture.
  • Water wisely: Water early in the morning or late in the evening to reduce evaporation. Focus on the root zone rather than wetting the foliage and use a watering can or drip line rather than a sprinkler.
  • Keep paths clear: Define your beds and paths clearly and keep paths weed‑free. This improves airflow around plants and makes the plot easier to manage.
  • Record your successes: Keep a diary of what you planted, where and when. Note the varieties that performed well and any problems you encountered so you can refine your plan each year.
